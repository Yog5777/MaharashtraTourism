    History of Ekvira Devi Temple, Dhule

    Aadishakti Ekvira Devi Temple is a prominent Hindu temple located in Dhule, Maharashtra. The Ekvira Devi Temple is only 1. 4 kilometers from the Dhule city's center point and it takes just only 5 minutes by vehicle or 15 minutes to by walk to reach the temple. According to epic of Mahabharata, the arrival of Ekvira Devi (incarnation of Renuka Devi) is that she appeared before the Pandavas during their Aranyavasam (exile period in forest) and asked them to build a temple for her. She also mentioned that she will bless him with a mighty boon only when they build the temple finished overnight. Pandavas agreed and built this beautiful temple overnight and they were blessed by the Ekvira Devi that they will not be discovered by anybody during their secret exile period. Later, according to carbon dating that this shrine was developed over two periods from 2nd Century BC and 2nd Century AD and from the 5th Century AD to the 10th Century.


    Aadishakti Ekvira Devi Temple is specially worshipped by the Aagri-Koli (Maharashtra's Fishermen) group of people and also many other high caste people like Daivadnya Brahminns and Kunbi caste worship the Ekvira Devi as their family deity. All occasions of Navarathri and Chaitri Navaratri mark a special rituals held in these Ekvira temples and huge crowd of devotees rush into the temple to have the mighty blessings from the Goddess Ekvira.


    Aadishakti Ekvira Devi Temple can be visited during timings 5:00 AM - 7:00 PM and special pujas or rituals held in the early morning and early evening like any other temple in India.
