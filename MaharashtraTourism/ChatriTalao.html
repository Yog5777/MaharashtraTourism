    Chatri Talao Amravati

    Chatri Talao Amravati, situated on the south eastern part of Amravati, was constructed dating back to the 18th century, makes for a fascinating heritage site with great historical significance. It is a water reservoir, built in the year 1888, on a small spring called ‘Kali Nadi’ with a purpose to supply drinking water to the city of Amravati. Presently, this scenically blessed reservoir gets stocked with the water supplied from the Upper Wardha Dam.


    Adorned with beautiful green landscape, this charming water body; Chatri Talao Amravati is the humble abode to numerous black ducks, that make for a soothing sight. Especially for the tourists and local people’s rejuvenation, a very pretty garden has been made near to it, which makes for a great place to enjoy picnics with family and friends. Flourished with manicured grasslands and colorful flowers, people visit here to laze around in the nature’s lap. Even a lone walk here is so soul nourishing and uplifting. For kids and fun lovers, there is boating facility also available which is quite engaging.


    To reach to this outstanding site, which is just 1 km from Dasturnagar Square on Malkhed Railway Road, one can take a private cab or auto, or even come by walking. Nearest Railway station to this place is Amravati railway station and nearest airport is Nagpur (150 km). Amravati is well connected by roads to other cities as well.


    History

    Chatri Talao is famous for the Reservoir that was built in 1888 and the only drinking source of water to Amaravati residents during that period. Chatri Talao is located on the Malked Railway Road which is 1 kilometre far from Dasturnagar Square and 4 kilometres far from the main city Amravati.


    The construction of the Chatri Talao took back in the 1888 AD as a small reservoir and built for the purpose of drinking water to the Amravati residents in and around. The Reservoir built on a small spring called Kali Nadi. The name explains as "Chatri" means 'Umbrella' and 'Talao' means Tank, so together it is an "Umbrella water tank" that stores water. Currently, this reservoir is no longer used for drinking purposes and it became as a tourists spot.


    This Reservoir is famous for the Migratory birds that visit from their far natural habitats to India in the months of Summer and Winter. One can see using their binoculars a huge flock of birds flying and swimming around. It is known that these birds migrate from far distant places to escape the severe weather conditions in their native places and India always being a diveserd weather conditions country, hosts these migratory birds every year. The other attractions in this place is the small garden and boating facilites available to tourists. The boating ride costs around Rs. 50-Rs. 100. The Sun rise and the sun set makes the view of the reservoir as more beautiful.


    Chatri Talao can be visited during timigns 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM and the entry ticket costs around INR 10. The best time to visit is in the months between October to March as there is a "Pakshi Jatra"(Birds Festival) which takes place here with the arrival of migratory birds. It takes around 30 minutes to 1 hour to visit this Talao but one can stay as long as they want until the closing time.


    Overview of Chatri Talao

    With a construction dating back to the 18th century, 1888 to be precise, the Chatri Talao is one of the oldest structures of Amravati. It holds significance in being a main source of water supply to the city of Amravati. For recreation, there are facilities of boating available here. This is one of the most serene places to bask in nature’s beauty in the outdoors of Amravati.
